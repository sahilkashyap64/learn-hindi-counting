Hindi Numbers 1–100: Patterns & Practice

Learn the key patterns and rules for Hindi numbers, then test yourself with the quiz below.

Patterns & Rules

1) 1 से 10: आधार (Base Numbers)

These are the crucial foundations for Hindi counting.

  • 1 → एक
  • 2 → दो
  • 3 → तीन
  • 4 → चार
  • 5 → पाँच
  • 6 → छः
  • 7 → सात
  • 8 → आठ
  • 9 → नौ
  • 10 → दस

2) 11 से 19: विशिष्ट (Unique Teens)

These have characteristic endings like -रह, -ह, -स.

  • 11 → ग्यारह
  • 12 → बारह
  • 13 → तेरह
  • 14 → चौदह
  • 15 → पंद्रह
  • 16 → सोलह
  • 17 → सत्रह
  • 18 → अट्ठारह
  • 19 → उन्नीस

Tip: These don’t follow a simple “10 + digit” rule, so memorize individually.

3) दशकों के मुख्य शब्द (Multiples of 10)

Each multiple of 10 has a unique term:

  • 20 → बीस
  • 30 → तीस
  • 40 → चालीस
  • 50 → पचास
  • 60 → साठ
  • 70 → सत्तर
  • 80 → अस्सी
  • 90 → नब्बे

4) 21 से 99: “दहाई + इकाई” (Combining Decade & Unit)

Most numbers from 21–99 are formed by combining the “decade base” (बीस, तीस, चालीस...) with a modified form of the digits (एक, दो, तीन...). For example, इक्कीस is basically इक + बीस, बाईस = बाय + बीस, etc.

Each decade has unique phonetic shifts. But the overall concept is [DecadeWord] + [DigitSound] → a blended word (e.g., इक + तीस = “इकतीस”).

5) 100 = सौ (and Beyond)

100 is सौ. Then 200 → दो सौ, 300 → तीन सौ, etc.
